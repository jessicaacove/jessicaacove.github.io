KUGADI

REDESIGNING ONBOARDING FOR SECURITY SOFTWARE

What is Kugadi?

Kugadi is software primarily for

security companies

that contract out employees to work at properties. It manages employee performance at the task level by correlatting each task with a scannable RFID tag.

The Brief

Work with company stakeholders to identify and solve a problem over a 10 day sprint.

The Plan

  • Research
  • Ideation
  • Prototype
  • Afterthoughts

Research


STAKEHOLDER TAKEAWAYS



Among the problem areas that the stakeholder identified, there was data to back-up that

97% of customers

are unable to complete onboarding without assistance, the average onboarding support phone call lasts

25-32 minutes


Specifically, customers struggle with understanding the nature and setup of

DARs

and

RFID tasks

USER RESEARCH

Who is onboarding?

A district manager or director, who trains guards and manages client assignments.

CURRENT ONBOARDING ANALYSIS

I began by watching the onboarding youtube video created by the company and creating a user flow outlining the onboarding process.

I then observed 5 users watch the same process. By weighing task impact with task difficulty, I decided to focus on the onboarding task of setting up DARs, or Daily Activity Reports.

The Problem


PAINS & GAINS




Through user observation and analysis, I identified the top pains and gains in the process of setting up Daily Activity Reports.


  • Unclear navigation
  • Undefined terminology
  • Decision paralysis

  • Quickly and easily set up a client account
  • Understand definitions of terminology
  • Identify options that best suit their needs
USER PERSONA AND USER JOURNEY

I created a user persona to represent the mental models and desires of my user group. I used it to refer back to and keep my design focused on the user.

I also created a user journey to help me to understand the context of the problem space.

Oscar is a district manager at a security company who trains guards and manages client assignments.

His professional goals include:


  • Manage employee tasks and productivity
  • Inform property owners of reported incidents
  • Transition to a new software system seamlessly with limited technical ability

Through his journey, Oscar comes across a few points of friction.



These enabled me to break down the problem into 4 "How might we" questions.


HOW MIGHT WE...

Direct users through a logical setup flow

Organize frequently used options in report setup

Provide explanations for terminology and features

Easily select email recipients for different reports and alerts

SOLUTION

Prototyping

LOW FIDELITY

The intention was to create a demo report that starts with default options showing. It would then change to reflect the options that you choose in real time.

"What is an Occurance Report?"

My major takeaway was that I needed to add definition tooltips that explained the terminology, so I quickly stuck on informative tabs using sticky notes.


"This is straight up overwhelming."

Users found my email delivery grid overwhelming, so I hoped to execute it better in mid-fidelity.

MID FIDELITY

In my Mid-fi, I was better able to communicate my progress bar.

I moved the DAR column in the email grid to the far left, so that users were immediately able to recognize that those were the emails that that they had already set up. I also simplified the select all and unselect all options, and users found it much less overwhelming. Overall, the response to these pages was good, however users were spending several minutes on average scanning the information on the page. It wasn't as quick and easy as I would like it to be.

HIGH FIDELITY

View report setup options in a live preview.

Select report delivery options and import recipients by CSV.

Preview and set up occurence reports.

Select occurence report recipients from email grid.

Afterthoughts

KEY LEARNINGS


I loved the technical constraints of working with a real company. I enjoyed breaking down the intracacies of a complex problem and finding a solution that works in the real world.

NEXT STEPS

That said, products are huge. I solved just a small piece of the onboarding, which is just a small piece of the overall product. I would love to continue design the rest of the onboarding process. Then I would like to test on real existing users at security companies and get their feedback.

SUCCESS & FAILURE METRICS
  • Fewer onboarding support calls
  • Higher onboarding task completion
  • Lower onboarding task times
