SPOTIFY

ADDING VIEW AND SEARCH BY LYRICS FEATURE

What is Spotify?

The world's leading music streaming app by market share.

I chose Spotify because I was initially attracted to the dark UI. They have 217 million monthly active users and climbing, and I welcomed the challenge of finding a problem with such a successful product and fitting it in to a feature-ful interface.

The Goal...

... is to

create a new feature
that meets user needs and integrates seamlessly with the existing product.

Research

Through background research, I learned that 99% of all available streaming comes from just 10% of songs.

I wondered if that 10% were those featured on playlists and if users had difficulty locating music outside that 10%.

Business Analysis


A Competitive Feature Comparison helped me to identify users' mental models and expectations, as well as identify opportunities for design.*

*This chart has been updated to reflect new information, see my afterthoughts.

I continued with a Market Positioning Chart. With my background research in mind, I wanted to see if the breadth of music offered and the organization of that music, played a role in positioning. If users only access 10% of music, do diverse offerings or organization play a role in a platform's popularity?

User Research

I did 10 interviews. I really had to dig deep; most users didn't think that they had any problems.

Remembering from my research that only 10% of music gets streamed, I asked my users about ways that they discover new music.

It stuck out that the majority discovered music by lyrics. I learned that lyrics are important to people. One user said,

“I like to see the lyrics because I like to sing along, and I don’t want to look stupid.”

Another expressed that Spotify showed lyrics for some songs, but they aren't always visible.

“The way that Spotify switches back-and-forth from showing the song lyrics to showing artist facts is annoying.”

Ideation

Throughout my process, I organized all my collected info and ideas in a living document, the Value Proposition Canvas.

This helped me to visualize how the features of the business met and caused the gains and pains of its users.

The need for users to find songs by audio was met by the business's partnership with Soundhound, a third-party audio recognition service.

I ideated based on the two remaining pain points to come up with a feature to meet users' needs.

Main Use Case

The listener is thinking of a line in an unknown song that they would like to play next. Once the song is playing, they realize they are unclear on a phrase in the song and would like to check the lyrics.

Afterthoughts

Key Learnings

I felt that I did a few things well.

  • Found a great pain point based on my background research
  • Identified an opportunity for design amongst competitors
Next Steps

There are a few things that I would like to improve on.

  • Expressing micro interactions and audio cues in different stages of prototyping was difficult
  • Figure out my preferred approach to atomic design; here i created atoms at the mid-fidelity level, detached them, and updated the symbols to high fidelity
Success and Failure Metrics

Indications of the success of this feature would be...

  • High task completion for the new Search by lyrics and Lyrics/Insights toggle features
  • Faster search result task times because users would now be able to search by a new metric
  • AND overall lower bounce rates from the app, as users would not be leaving the app to search for lyrics

I believe that this feature would be successful, but I won't find out unless Spotify decides to implement something similar.

P.S.

About 2/3 of the way through my process, I discovered that Apple Music had recently released a "search by lyrics" feature. The competitor implementation validates that this is a user need in the streaming market.

I updated my Feature Comparison Chart, accordingly.
